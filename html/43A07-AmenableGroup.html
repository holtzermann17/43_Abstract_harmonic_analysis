amenable group


Let G be a locally compact group and L∞⁢(G) be the Banach space of all essentially bounded functions G→ℝ with respect to the Haar measure.

Definition 1.

A linear functional on L∞⁢(G) is called a mean if it maps the constant function f⁢(g)=1 to 1 and non-negative functions to non-negative numbers.

Definition 2.

Let Lg be the left action of g∈G on f∈L∞⁢(G), i.e. (Lg⁢f)⁢(h)=f⁢(g-1⁢h). Then, a mean μ is said to be left invariant if μ⁢(Lg⁢f)=μ⁢(f) for all g∈G and f∈L∞⁢(G). Similarly, right invariant if μ⁢(Rg⁢f)=μ⁢(f), where Rg is the right action (Rg⁢f)⁢(h)=f⁢(h⁢g).

Definition 3.

A locally compact group G is amenable if there is a left (or right) invariant mean on L∞⁢(G).

Example 1 (Amenable groups)

All finite groups and all abelian groups are amenable. Compact groups are amenable as the Haar measure is an (unique) invariant mean.

Example 2 (Non-amenable groups)

If a group contains a free (non-abelian) subgroup on two generators then it is not amenable.
